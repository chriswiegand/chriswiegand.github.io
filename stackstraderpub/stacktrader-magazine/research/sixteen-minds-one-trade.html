16 Minds, One Trade

The Mixture-of-Experts architecture behind Stack$Trader: how 16 specialist AI agents each analyse a different dimension of the market, vote on every proposed trade, and why their disagreement carries as much signal as their agreement.

By Stack$Trader Research Team  ·  March 2026  ·  12 min read

A single expert, no matter how skilled, has blind spots. A committee of experts with diverse specialisations — where each member is accountable for a specific domain and can flag concerns outside their colleagues' purview — is more robust. This is the intuition behind the Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) architecture at the heart of Stack$Trader.

The system employs 16 specialist AI agents. Each produces a normalised score between -1 and +1. These scores are aggregated by a Supervisor module using a weighted voting rule. A trade is only executed when the weighted composite score clears a configurable threshold — and two structural mechanisms, a disagreement penalty and a conviction bonus, modulate the composite to encode the quality of consensus, not just its direction.

The 16 agents

01
TechnicalAgent
Price Action  ·  Weight: 0.10–0.60
RSI overbought/oversold, Bollinger Band positioning, MACD crossovers, EMA stack alignment, percentage change thresholds. The primary entry-timing filter — highest weight in conservative profiles.
02
OptionsPricingAgent
Volatility Surface  ·  Weight: 0.30–0.55
Implied Volatility rank (IVR), volatility regime detection, vol stability scoring, surface skew analysis. Identifies when premiums are rich enough to justify selling — the VRP harvesting signal.
03
FundamentalsAgent
Valuation  ·  Weight: 0.15–0.30
SMA stretch (price deviation from long-term average), volume anomaly detection, maximum drawdown magnitude. Flags when a stock is over-extended and prone to mean-reversion that would threaten open positions.
04
MacroAgent
Regime  ·  Weight: 0.10–0.25
VIX level and VIX/VIX3M spread (term structure), broad market trend, sector momentum. Provides macro context — distinguishes healthy high-vol from crisis high-vol, which require opposite responses.
05
EarningsAgent
Event Risk  ·  Weight: 0.25–0.60
Gaussian decay function centred on earnings dates, post-earnings drift scoring. The primary catastrophic-scenario prevention agent. Highest weight in conservative profiles. Consistently the most impactful filter in ablation testing.
06
CorrelationAgent
Cross-Asset  ·  Weight: 0.05–0.20
Cross-ticker correlation regime detection, dispersion scoring across the portfolio universe. Identifies when normally uncorrelated assets have become correlated — a leading indicator of systemic stress.
07
SentimentAgent
NLP / News  ·  Weight: 0.15–0.40
News headline sentiment scoring, social media signal aggregation. Detects narrative momentum — when sentiment shifts, price often follows, making it a leading indicator for vol regime transitions.
08
AnalystConsensusAgent
Sell-Side  ·  Weight: 0.05–0.15
Consensus ratings aggregation, revision momentum tracking. Lowest-weighted agent by design — sell-side consensus is a lagging indicator but adds a modest signal for fundamentally challenged tickers.
09
HMMAgent
Hidden Markov Regime  ·  Weight: 0.20–0.40
3-state Gaussian HMM posterior probabilities for Grind (State 0), Mean-Reversion (State 1), and Crisis (State 2). Score is P(grind) – P(crisis), weighted by state transition stability. The regime-awareness backbone of the system.
10
MicrostructureAgent
Order Flow  ·  Weight: 0.10–0.45
Order Flow Imbalance (OFI) proxy, liquidity exhaustion detection, intraday momentum. Requires intraday data to be effective — daily-bar variants showed zero edge in controlled experiments.
11
FINNAgent
Volatility Surface Arbitrage  ·  Weight: 0.10–0.60
Volatility surface mispricing detection — identifies when implied vol across strikes and expirations is inconsistent with no-arbitrage constraints. Term structure arbitrage signals. The vol-surface intelligence layer.
12
AnomalyAgent
Outlier Detection  ·  Weight: 0.05–0.30
Variational Autoencoder (VAE) reconstruction error scoring. The VAE learns a compressed representation of "normal" market conditions; high reconstruction error signals distributional anomaly — conditions outside the training distribution.
13
DealerPositioningAgent
Dealer Flow  ·  Weight: 0.15–0.40
Gamma Exposure (GEX): 40% weight. Vanna exposure (cross-Greek vol-delta sensitivity): 30%. Dark Index (DIX) from FINRA dark pool data: 30%. The 13th and most recently added agent — reads the footprints of market makers in the options chain. See the full Theory piece on dealer positioning.
14
SoftTechnicalAgent (Daily)
Multi-Timeframe Technical  ·  Weight: 0.00–0.25
Continuous daily trend scoring — a softer version of binary technical rules that scores the degree of trend conviction, not just its direction. Provides momentum context at the daily resolution.
15
SoftTechnicalAgent (60min)
Multi-Timeframe Technical  ·  Weight: 0.00–0.20
Hourly momentum scoring for entry timing. Allows the system to find the better entry point within a day that has already cleared the daily-resolution filters.
16
SoftTechnicalAgent (5min)
Multi-Timeframe Technical  ·  Weight: 0.00–0.15
5-minute micro-momentum scoring for precise entry timing. Lowest-weighted agent — its contribution is execution refinement, not trade selection. Works in conjunction with the Charm-Aware timing model.

The Supervisor: aggregation with intelligence

The Supervisor takes the 16 agent scores and computes a weighted composite. The decision rule is simple in principle:

Entry allowed ⟺ Σ(w_i × s_i) / Σ(w_i) > τ

Where w_i is agent i's weight, s_i ∈ [-1, +1] is its score, and τ is the character-preset threshold. But two mechanisms make this more than a weighted average:

Disagreement Penalty: When agents produce similar numerical means but with high variance — for example, TechnicalAgent at +1.0 and MacroAgent at -1.0 — the composite score is penalised. High agent disagreement is itself a signal of uncertain conditions where staying out is the right choice. This penalty is one of the primary whipsaw-reduction mechanisms in the system.

Conviction Bonus: When agents achieve high consensus (low cross-agent variance), the composite is amplified. The bonus isolates genuinely high-probability setups from borderline ones, ensuring that the most favourable conditions receive proportionally larger position allocations.
