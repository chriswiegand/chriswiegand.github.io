How to read this table

OOS Sharpe is the out-of-sample Sharpe ratio — the only number that matters for evaluating whether a strategy has genuine edge. It is computed on data the strategy never saw during its development or parameter optimisation.

IS Sharpe is the in-sample Sharpe — included for context and to compute WFE, but not the ranking criterion. A high IS Sharpe with a low OOS Sharpe is the classical fingerprint of overfitting.

WFE (Walk-Forward Efficiency) = OOS Sharpe ÷ IS Sharpe. Values above 1.0 indicate that the strategy generalised well to unseen data. Values substantially above 1.0 suggest the out-of-sample period was structurally more favourable than the training period — a positive signal. Values substantially below 1.0 flag potential overfitting.
