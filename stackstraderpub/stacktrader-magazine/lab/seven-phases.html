The Lab

Seven Phases: A Chronicle

From the first line of synthetic data generation code to a live-broker-integrated 16-agent system — the complete development history of Stack$Trader, told phase by phase.

By Stack$Trader Research Team  ·  March 2026  ·  12 min read

Stack$Trader was not designed upfront and then built. It was discovered — through a sequence of experiments, dead ends, breakthroughs, and refinements that each changed what came next. This is the honest account of that process.

1 Foundation
Synthetic Data, DataProvider Protocol & Architecture
54,000 synthetic contracts DataProvider abstraction

The project began with a problem: no historical options chain data, and no way to get it cheaply at the scale needed for rigorous backtesting. The solution was synthetic generation — a generate_synthetic_chain() function that iterates over 900+ trading days per ticker, producing roughly 54,000 synthetic contracts by computing theoretical prices from realised volatility, time decay, and the risk-free rate. Output was exported to local PyArrow Parquet arrays, bypassing all external API limits.

Alongside the data, Phase 1 established the DataProvider protocol — an abstraction layer that enforces a strict interface over data ingestion so the core strategy engine never interacts directly with an API endpoint. This architectural decision paid dividends in every subsequent phase: swapping data sources required no changes to the strategy engine itself.

2 Experiment
The 34-Strategy Experiment & HMM Breakthrough
34 strategies tested 3-state HMM Sharpe 8.2 at 20-day hold

Phase 2 was pure quantitative research. Thirty-four strategy variations were tested across five experiments on a 5-year SPY dataset. The Hidden Markov Model, trained on daily SPY returns, classified every trading day into one of three hidden states: Grind (812 days, 66%), Mean-Reversion (213 days, 17%), and Crisis (210 days, 17%).

Experiment A — switching from binary HMM classification to posterior probability weighting — improved Sharpe from 2.67 to 4.07. Experiment E — testing the scaling of edge with holding period — found that theta decay in the Grind regime scales linearly with time, reaching Sharpe 8.2 at 20-day hold (p=0.0005). These two findings became the permanent structural foundation of everything that followed.

Equally important: Experiment C (microstructure from daily bars) was a complete failure. Every variation showed no edge. This negative result was as valuable as the positive ones — it prevented an entire dead-end development branch.

3 Ensemble
Mixture-of-Experts Architecture & Multi-Ticker Validation
12 agents deployed 41 tickers Character presets

Phase 3 expanded the single-signal HMM approach into a full Mixture-of-Experts ensemble. The original 12 agents were built and integrated: Technical, OptionsPricing, Fundamentals, Macro, Earnings, Correlation, Sentiment, AnalystConsensus, HMM, Microstructure, FINN (volatility surface arbitrage), and Anomaly (VAE). The Supervisor module with weighted voting, disagreement penalty, and conviction bonus was designed and implemented.

Character presets — Conservative, Moderate, Aggressive, Gold Standard, IncomesFocus — enabled the same underlying architecture to serve different risk profiles without requiring separate codebases. The 41-ticker universe was assembled and validated, confirming that the structural edge generalised across asset classes (equities, ETFs, precious metals).

4 Validation
Microstructure, Overnight Decomposition & 10-Test Validation Battery
SPA p=0.000 WFE 3.886 10 independent tests

Phase 4 was the statistical validation campaign. The 10-test battery was designed and executed: SPA, theta-normalised permutation, walk-forward efficiency, Masters permutation, overnight/intraday decomposition (Lou et al. 2019), DFA Hurst exponent, spectral density, Monte Carlo stress test, earnings cluster stress test, and cross-bucket generalisation.

The most significant findings: SPA p=0.000 (real alpha, not selection bias), directional permutation p=1.000 (zero timing component — all edge is structural theta), and WFE of 3.886 (no overfitting, out-of-sample exceeds in-sample). The validation battery is now rerun automatically as part of every major parameter update.

5 Intelligence
Alternative Data, Multi-Timeframe Resonance & Gold Standard Profile
16 agents Gold Standard profile Multi-TF resonance

Phase 5 completed the 16-agent ensemble by adding the DealerPositioningAgent (GEX, Vanna, DIX), three SoftTechnicalAgent instances at daily, 60-minute, and 5-minute resolutions, and enhancing the FINN and AnomalyAgent with production-ready implementations. The multi-timeframe resonance filter — which requires alignment across daily, hourly, and 5-minute technical scores before entry — became a key component of the Gold Standard profile.

The SchwabOptionProvider was integrated, enabling live option chain data from the Schwab API. The standalone recorder, which sweeps the option chain daily for all 41 tickers and archives strikes and Greeks, was deployed as an always-on background service. Phase 5 closed when the full Gold Standard profile passed its initial validation run.

6 Optimisation
Grid Search, Edge-In Sampling & Per-Ticker Optimisation
923 strategies 324 param combos 2D RBF interpolation

Phase 6 was the optimisation and scale-testing phase. The edge-in sampling algorithm replaced the previous sparse discovery approach, selecting 20 calls and 4 puts per ticker by starting at the extremes of the DTE/moneyness grid and working inward — maximising information density at the wings where vol surface curvature matters most. The chain reconstructor was upgraded from 1D cubic spline to 2D RBF (Radial Basis Function) interpolation over a DTE-by-moneyness surface, enabling accurate Greek computation across the full chain.

The 324-parameter grid search across 40+ tickers with 3-fold walk-forward validation produced the 923-strategy leaderboard. Per-ticker weight optimisation via scipy.optimize differential evolution identified ticker-specific agent configurations that outperform uniform weights. Phase 6 closed with the per-ticker assignment CSV and the confirmed Gold Standard WFE of 3.886.

7 Live
Dealer Positioning, Live Integration & Continuous Improvement
GEX / Vanna / DIX Live kill switch 119 tests passing

Phase 7 is the current and ongoing phase. The DealerPositioningAgent was implemented — computing Gamma Exposure (GEX), Vanna, and Charm directly from the Schwab live option chain, and integrating Dark Index (DIX) data from FINRA dark pool FINRA reports. The Charm-Aware execution timing model uses |charm| thresholds to schedule entries: high charm (>0.03) triggers a 45-minute delay to avoid hedging flow; medium charm a 20-minute delay; low charm proceeds immediately.

A live trading kill switch with environment variable safeguards was deployed, preventing accidental live execution during development. Three architecture design documents — live broker integration, real-time P&L alerting, and risk guardrails — define the path to capital deployment. The dual-AI collaboration framework between Claude (implementation lead) and Gemini (research lead) was formalised. 119 tests currently pass. Phase 7 continues.
