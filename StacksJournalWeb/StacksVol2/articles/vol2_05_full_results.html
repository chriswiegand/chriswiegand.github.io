35 Tickers, 80 Cycles,
One Clear Winner

LRCX leads at fitness 1.39. NOT() gates dominate a third of the portfolio. IV rank emerges as the single most impactful indicator. The framework is validated at scale.

The first full-scale guided evolution run completed in 2.54 hours: 80 cycles across 35 tickers, 50 generations per cycle, population 120. Of those 35 tickers, 34 achieved positive fitness. Pass 2 improved 13 of 35, confirming that additional evolutionary passes find better trees.

Stack$Trader Research TeamWHIPSAW  ·  March 2026
14 min read

The first full-scale guided evolution run completed today: 80 cycles across 35 tickers, 50 generations per cycle, population 120, in 2.54 hours and zero LLM tokens. Of those 35 tickers, 34 achieved positive fitness (defined as ΔSharpe relative to always-on, weighted by walk-forward efficiency). LRCX leads the leaderboard at 1.3914, followed by NVO at 1.0351 and TSM at 0.9686—all three improved on their second evolutionary pass. The portfolio-level finding is unmistakable: NOT() gate structures, which veto covered call sales during unfavorable conditions rather than selecting for favorable ones, dominate 12 of 35 tickers and achieve 63% higher average fitness than single-leaf trees. Implied volatility rank (iv_rank_high) is the single most frequently discovered indicator, appearing independently in four separate tickers’ champion trees.

Run Configuration

The run was configured for the full PORTFOLIO_SHARES universe of 40 tickers. Five were skipped due to insufficient daily bar data: FNMA, GRAL, LTH, CPER, and VRT. The remaining 35 tickers were processed in round-robin order over two full passes: cycles 1–40 (Pass 1, warm-started from production veto champions plus top-5 variant trees) and cycles 41–80 (Pass 2, randomly initialized). The --skip-llm flag was set, making this a pure genetic baseline—no Sonnet interpretation or redirection.

The distribution is right-skewed: 10 tickers (29%) exceeded 0.5 fitness, but only 2 (6%) broke the 1.0 threshold. The tail is LRCX at 1.39 and NVO at 1.04. Tree Pattern Analysis: NOT() Gates Dominate

Twelve of 35 tickers converged to NOT() gate structures, averaging fitness 0.557 versus 0.341 for single-leaf trees—a 63% advantage. The genetic algorithm consistently discovers that knowing when not to sell outperforms knowing when to sell.

The tree pattern distribution breaks into three structural families. NOT() gates (12 tickers, 34%) negate a single indicator or compound condition. Single-leaf trees (21 tickers, 60%) use a bare indicator as a positive selection filter. AND() compound trees (2 tickers, 6%) combine multiple conditions into a conjunctive gate. No tickers converged to OR() structures.

The performance gap is meaningful. NOT() gates average 0.557 fitness versus 0.341 for single leaves—the difference holds across the fitness distribution, not just in the top tier. Three of the top 5 ranked tickers use NOT() gates. The explanation is structural: for covered call selling, the downside risk is upside truncation (assignment at the strike), while the upside benefit is premium capture. A veto system that blocks trades during dangerous regimes (high IV rank, strong trends, earnings proximity) preserves optionality during the periods where the underlying is most likely to run, while allowing premium harvesting during the calmer majority of the time.

The genetic algorithm, searching blindly, independently converges to the same economic truth across tickers with very different fundamentals.

The IV Rank Pattern

Three of the four iv_rank_high appearances use it in a NOT() gate: NVDA, TSM, and GLD all evolved NOT(iv_rank_high), meaning they veto covered call sales when IV rank is elevated. This is counterintuitive at first glance—higher IV means fatter premium. But the data reveals the tradeoff: high IV rank environments correlate with large directional moves that cause assignment risk. The optimal strategy is to sit out the storm and sell calls during normal volatility, where premium is thinner but collection is more reliable. OPEN is the exception, using iv_rank_high as a positive condition inside its AND/OR compound tree—consistent with its high-beta, low-price characteristic where high IV signals peak-premium extraction opportunities.

Regime Clusters

Grouping tickers by the signal category of their champion tree reveals portfolio-level regime dependencies.

Volatility-Aware (NVDA, TSM, GLD, IBIT)

All four converged to NOT() gates over volatility indicators: three use NOT(iv_rank_high), one uses NOT(adx_strong). These are high-beta or macro-sensitive names where elevated volatility signals directional risk. Average veto rate: 37%. Average fitness: 0.57.

Premium Harvesters (NVO, IBM, HON)

All three use bare vrp (volatility risk premium) as their champion tree. These are stable, dividend-paying names where the edge comes from consistently harvesting the gap between implied and realized volatility. Average veto rate: 22%. Average fitness: 0.81.

Momentum / Structure (TSLA, SOFI)

Both converged to support_resist, vetoing covered call sales when price is at key support or resistance levels. Average veto rate: 40%. The high veto rate reflects these names’ tendency to consolidate at technical levels before breakout moves.

Earnings Specialists (CEG, LMT, MAR, COIN)

Four tickers discovered earnings-related trees. CEG found NOT(earn_avoid)—the contrarian signal to sell premium near earnings, exploiting elevated IV crush. LMT and MAR found pre_earnings, selling into IV expansion. COIN found AND(aroon_bullish, earn_avoid), the only compound positive-selection tree related to earnings.

Limitations

This run establishes a pure-genetic baseline, not a finished result. Four limitations merit explicit acknowledgment.

No LLM guidance. The --skip-llm flag was set throughout. The core value proposition of the guided evolution framework—Sonnet interpreting evolved patterns and redirecting the search—is untested at scale. The LLM may or may not close the gap with production champions.

No cross-pollination. Production’s veto evolver shares top trees between tickers, creating a network effect where a discovery in one ticker accelerates convergence in structurally similar ones. Guided evolution processes each ticker independently. Adding cross-pollination is a known enhancement.

Two passes only. Production champions were evolved over 15+ passes. With only 2 passes, the guided evolution results represent an early snapshot, not a converged endpoint. The 37% improvement rate from Pass 1 to Pass 2 implies that additional passes would continue finding better trees, with diminishing returns.

Daily bars only. The WFE backtests use daily bars, where trade frequency is low and validation windows contain few datapoints. The 3-fold WFE helps, but statistical power for distinguishing closely-performing trees is limited. The intraday (5-minute) frequency, where trade counts are an order of magnitude higher, would provide sharper discrimination.

What Comes Next

The immediate next run will enable Sonnet interpretation and redirection (--model sonnet, removing --skip-llm) with the same 80-cycle configuration. The comparison between this pure-genetic baseline and the LLM-guided results will quantify the marginal value of Sonnet’s structural insights. If guided fitness exceeds production for any ticker, scripts/star/sync_production.py --mode review --confirm will promote those champions into live trading via veto_champions.json.
