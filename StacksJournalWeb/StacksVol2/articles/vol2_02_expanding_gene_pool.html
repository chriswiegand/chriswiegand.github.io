Expanding the
Gene Pool

The genetic algorithm’s DNA pool grows from 45 to 63 triggers — adding sentiment, calendar effects, momentum derivatives, and cross-asset signals

Eighteen new boolean triggers enter the evolutionary search space, drawn from four previously untapped information sources. Both overnight and intraday evolution runs complete with 100% positive ΔSharpe across all tickers. The AVWAP family dominates champion trees at both resolutions.

Stack$Trader Research TeamWHIPSAW  ·  March 2026
10 min read

The veto evolution system’s DNA pool—the set of atomic boolean triggers that the genetic algorithm can compose into gate trees—expanded from 45 to 63 triggers today, and the first full evolution runs with the enlarged pool have already completed. After 8 passes on the overnight daily system (10 hours) and 6 passes on the intraday 5-minute system (8 hours), every ticker in both portfolios shows positive ΔSharpe: an average of +0.68 across 35 daily tickers and +2.05 across 41 intraday tickers. The new material gives the algorithm genetic building blocks it never had before—sentiment polarity, options expiration timing, MACD acceleration, and Treasury bond divergence—and the early champion trees are already weaving them into their logic.

Why the DNA Pool Matters

The veto evolution system, implemented across scripts/veto_evolver.py and scripts/intraday_veto_deep.py, breeds gate trees through mutation, crossover, and tournament selection. Each tree is a boolean expression composed of leaf triggers connected by AND, OR, and NOT operators. When the tree evaluates to TRUE, the system vetoes the covered call for that bar; when FALSE, the call is written. The quality of the evolved trees is bounded by the quality of the available leaves.

The TRIGGER_MODES registry in scripts/harvest_simple_triggers.py defines every trigger the algorithm can use. Excluding the always_on baseline, the pool constitutes COMBO_TRIGGERS—the actual DNA the genetic operators draw from when constructing random subtrees, mutating leaf nodes, or splicing crossover points. A larger pool does not guarantee better trees, but it expands the space of possible solutions. An algorithm searching 63-choose-5 leaf combinations has access to 7.6 million five-leaf trees compared to 1.2 million with 45 triggers. More importantly, the new triggers encode information that was previously invisible to the gate trees entirely.

The Five New Trigger Groups

The 18 new triggers fall into five groups, each addressing a distinct category of information that the original DNA pool could not express. All implementations follow the existing IndicatorAlpha or specialised alpha-class patterns, ensuring compatibility with the gate tree evaluator without modification to the evolution infrastructure.

Group 1: Unused Engine Columns (5 triggers)

The engine’s compute_indicators() function already calculates several columns that had no corresponding trigger in the DNA pool. These were computed every backtest run but never exposed to the genetic algorithm—wasted work and wasted information.

Unused Engine Column Triggers
Trigger Column Condition Covered Call Rationale
vix_move_danger vix_move_divergence > 1.0 (Z-score) Equity vol exceeding bond stress—panic concentrated in stocks
gex_negative gex_regime_score < −0.3 Dealer short gamma amplifies moves—dangerous for short premium
gap_cluster gap_frequency_20 > 25% Frequent overnight gaps indicate event-driven regime
hurst_accel hurst_accel > 0.1 (Δ) Hurst exponent changing rapidly—regime character in flux
iv_vel_rising iv_velocity > 0.0 Implied vol expanding—premium rising but assignment risk increasing
Source: scripts/harvest_simple_triggers.py lines 821–857

The gex_negative trigger is particularly interesting for covered call timing. When dealers are net short gamma (GEX regime score below −0.3), their delta hedging amplifies price moves in both directions. Writing a covered call into a short-gamma environment means the underlying is more likely to make an outsized move through the strike. The old DNA pool had no way to express this risk; the engine computed gex_regime_score every day, but the gate tree could not see it.

Group 2: Perplexity Sentiment (4 triggers)

Stack$Trader’s Perplexity-sourced sentiment pipeline generates daily narrative scores for each ticker: a sentiment polarity score, a confidence level, a catalyst count, and a regime trajectory. Until now, these influenced the MiE (Mixture-of-Experts) agents through continuous weighting but were never discretised into veto triggers.

Sentiment Triggers
Trigger Column Condition Covered Call Rationale
sent_bearish sent_score < −0.3 Strongly negative narrative—potential for further downside
sent_bullish sent_score + sent_confidence > 0.3 AND > 0.5 Confident bullish narrative—call may be assigned if rally extends
catalyst_rich sent_catalyst_count ≥ 3 Multiple upcoming catalysts—high event density increases vol
regime_deteriorating sent_regime_score < −0.5 Narrative regime worsening—trending deterioration in outlook
Source: scripts/harvest_simple_triggers.py lines 942–957

The sent_bullish trigger is a compound condition requiring both positive polarity (> 0.3) and high confidence (> 0.5). This dual gate prevents noisy, low-conviction bullish readings from triggering a veto. The rationale for vetoing on bullish sentiment may seem counterintuitive for a covered call writer—why avoid writing calls when sentiment is positive? Because a confident bullish consensus increases the probability of an upside move through the strike, leading to assignment and lost upside. The gate tree can evolve to veto during these windows, preserving equity appreciation.

Group 3: Calendar Effects (3 triggers)

Options markets are not calendar-neutral. The expiration cycle, day-of-week effects, and holiday schedules create structural patterns in volatility, volume, and dealer hedging flows. Three triggers encode the most well-documented calendar effects.

Calendar Triggers
Trigger Column Condition Covered Call Rationale
opex_week is_opex_week = 1 Gamma/pin risk elevated during monthly OpEx; max pain dynamics distort price
friday is_friday = 1 Weekend theta decay opportunity—two days of decay for one day of risk
monday is_monday = 1 Post-weekend gap risk; vol often reprices after weekend news absorption
Source: scripts/harvest_simple_triggers.py lines 1017–1027

OpEx week (opex_week) is the most important of these. During the week of monthly options expiration (typically the third Friday), open interest concentrations create pin risk around popular strikes, and dealer gamma hedging can dampen or amplify moves unpredictably. A covered call written on Monday of OpEx week with a Friday expiry may behave very differently from the same call written the week before. The algorithm can now learn this distinction rather than treating all weeks as equivalent.

Group 4: Momentum Derivatives (4 triggers)

The original DNA pool included macd (MACD histogram positive) as a single trigger, but lacked any measure of how momentum is changing. The new group adds second-derivative signals—acceleration and deceleration of existing momentum indicators.

Momentum Derivative Triggers
Trigger Column Condition Covered Call Rationale
macd_accel macd_accel > 0.0 Momentum expanding—trend strengthening, assignment risk rising
macd_decel macd_accel < 0.0 Momentum fading—favourable for premium collection
roc_decel roc_decel < 0.0 10-day rate of change slowing—trend exhaustion, mean reversion likely
vol_drought vol_drought_ratio < 0.5 Volume below 50% of 20d avg—disinterest / low liquidity regime
Source: scripts/harvest_simple_triggers.py lines 1063–1077

The distinction between macd_accel and macd_decel gives the genetic algorithm access to a critical timing concept: momentum that is positive but decelerating is a very different environment from momentum that is positive and accelerating. A covered call writer wants to capture premium in the former (the rally is losing steam, strike is less likely to be breached) and potentially veto in the latter (the rally is strengthening). The old macd trigger could not distinguish these regimes.

Group 5: Cross-Asset TLT Signals (2 triggers)

The final group introduces cross-asset information via TLT, the iShares 20+ Year Treasury Bond ETF (exchange-traded fund). Bond market behaviour provides a macro signal that equity-only indicators cannot capture.

Cross-Asset TLT Triggers
Trigger Column Condition Covered Call Rationale
tlt_divergence tlt_divergence = 1 TLT moving opposite to ticker—flight to safety or risk-on divergence
tlt_corr_neg tlt_corr_negative = 1 Rolling correlation with TLT negative—bond/equity decoupling
Source: scripts/harvest_simple_triggers.py lines 1172–1178

When TLT rallies while equities fall, capital is flowing from risk assets into safe havens—a classic flight-to-safety regime. The tlt_divergence trigger fires in this condition, giving the gate tree a way to express macro risk aversion. Conversely, when bond and equity correlations decouple entirely (tlt_corr_neg), the historical relationship between the two asset classes has broken down, suggesting a regime shift that could make either directional bet unreliable. For a covered call writer, both signals suggest elevated uncertainty that may warrant vetoing.

Evolution Results: First Run with 63 Triggers

Both evolution processes were launched by Gemini before a 5-day API quota timeout and ran to completion unattended. The overnight system completed 8 passes in 10 hours; the intraday system completed 6 passes in 8 hours. The results are unambiguous: every ticker improved.

The intraday system’s 3x higher average ΔSharpe (+2.05 vs. +0.68) reflects the higher data density of 5-minute bars—roughly 78 bars per trading day versus one daily bar—which gives the fitness function more granular information about when veto decisions add value. This is consistent with prior passes and is not attributable to the DNA expansion alone.

A genetic algorithm with 63 triggers can express veto logic that was structurally impossible with 45—not merely harder to find, but inexpressible within the old search space.

Structural Observations

Several patterns in the results warrant attention, though none should be treated as definitive conclusions from a single evolution run.

AVWAP triggers dominate. The anchored VWAP triggers (avwap_sl_below, avwap_sh_near, avwap_sh_above, avwap_upper_half, avwap_lower_half) added in Phase 5 appear in champion trees at both resolutions with high frequency. LMT, VOO, INTC, and HON all feature AVWAP leaves prominently. This continues a trend observed in prior passes and validates the statistical analysis that preceded their addition.

Veto rates cluster around 30–40%. The median daily veto rate is 35.4%; the median intraday rate is 33.3%. Trees that veto too aggressively (above 50%) leave too little time in the market to collect premium. Trees that veto too rarely (below 15%) add minimal value over the always-on baseline. The genetic algorithm converges on a sweet spot where roughly one-third of bars are filtered out, concentrating premium collection into the most favourable regime.

New triggers have not yet appeared in champions. The 18 Phase 6 triggers are now in the DNA pool, but the passes completed so far (8 overnight, 6 intraday) may not have explored enough generations for the new material to propagate into champion trees. The genetic operators—random leaf generation draws uniformly from COMBO_TRIGGERS via _random_leaf() in scripts/gate_combinator.py—will introduce them stochastically. With 63 triggers, any single trigger has a 1.6% chance of being selected for a given leaf, down from 2.2% with 45 triggers. It may take several more passes before the new triggers have sufficient selection pressure to displace established leaves.

This is expected and not a concern. The DNA pool expansion is an investment in future generations, not a guarantee of immediate adoption. The next evolution passes (starting with Pass 9 daily and Pass 7 intraday) will benefit from the expanded search space as mutation and crossover gradually introduce the new material into the population.

Implementation Details

The 18 triggers are implemented as factory functions in scripts/harvest_simple_triggers.py (lines 821–1178), following four patterns depending on the data source:

IndicatorAlpha (Group 1, 5 triggers): Direct threshold on an existing compute_indicators() column. No additional computation required at evaluation time.

_SentimentAlpha (Group 2, 4 triggers): Reads from the Perplexity sentiment daily index parquet, joined by date. Supports compound conditions via optional column2/op2/threshold2b parameters, as used by sent_bullish.

_CalendarAlpha (Group 3, 3 triggers): Computes date-derived features (day of week, OpEx week detection) and injects them as columns. The OpEx calculation identifies the third Friday of each month and flags the Monday–Friday of that week.

_MomentumAlpha and _CrossAssetAlpha (Groups 4–5, 6 triggers): Compute derived columns from existing bar data. Momentum triggers calculate second derivatives of MACD histogram and rate of change. Cross-asset triggers load TLT bar data via LocalBarProvider and compute rolling correlation and divergence flags against the target ticker.

All 18 triggers are registered in TRIGGER_MODES and automatically included in COMBO_TRIGGERS (which is defined as all keys in TRIGGER_MODES excluding always_on). No changes were required to scripts/veto_evolver.py, scripts/intraday_veto_deep.py, or scripts/gate_combinator.py—the evolution infrastructure picks up new triggers from the registry without modification.

Limitations

The expanded DNA pool introduces three risks worth monitoring.

First, search space dilution. With 63 triggers, the algorithm must explore a larger combinatorial space. If the population size and generation count are not scaled accordingly, useful new triggers may not receive sufficient exposure before the evolution budget expires. The current 10-hour budget produced 8 passes across 35 tickers, averaging roughly 21 minutes per ticker per pass. Whether this is sufficient for 63-trigger exploration is an empirical question that will be answered by monitoring whether new triggers appear in champions over the next several passes.

Second, sentiment data sparsity. The Perplexity sentiment pipeline has been running since early February 2026, providing approximately five weeks of daily data. The walk-forward training windows span years of historical bar data, but sentiment columns will be NaN for the vast majority of that history. The _SentimentAlpha class handles missing data by treating NaN as a non-triggered state (the trigger evaluates to FALSE), but this means sentiment triggers can only influence the most recent fold windows. Their true value will not be testable until several months of sentiment data have accumulated.

Third, cross-asset data dependency. The TLT triggers require TLT bar data to be available in the LocalBarProvider. If TLT data is missing for a given date range, these triggers silently produce FALSE for all bars in that range. This is a soft failure mode that could mask the triggers’ actual value during backtesting.

What Comes Next

The immediate next step is to launch Pass 9 (overnight) and Pass 7 (intraday) with the full 63-trigger DNA pool. These passes will benefit from the champions already discovered in passes 1–8, using them as seed parents for the next generation while also introducing new random trees drawn from the expanded trigger set.

Longer term, the DNA pool expansion establishes a pattern for systematic growth: identify information sources the gate trees cannot currently access, discretise them into boolean triggers with clear covered call rationale, register them in TRIGGER_MODES, and let the genetic algorithm determine their value through selection pressure. The next candidates under consideration include intraday volume profile triggers (volume at price levels), dark pool print detection, and Federal Reserve meeting calendar effects. Each would follow the same integration pattern: factory function, threshold logic, registry entry, and evolutionary discovery.
