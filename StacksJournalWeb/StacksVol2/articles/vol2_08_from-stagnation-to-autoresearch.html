From Stagnation to AutoResearch

Three Generations of Evolutionary Failure

Training fitness of 3.93 produced forward PNL of -$35,827. The conclusion is stark: the genetic algorithm is optimizing the wrong thing. This article documents the failures of V5 through V7 and why the path forward runs through automated research.

ChrisEngineer  ·  March 14, 2026
14 min read

Training fitness of 3.93 produced forward PNL of -$35,827. The correlation between the two was r=0.108 (p=0.539) — statistically indistinguishable from random. After three major evolutionary revisions, five A/B tests, and a 35×35 cross-ticker matrix, the conclusion is stark: the genetic algorithm (GA) is optimizing the wrong thing. V5 through V7 each solved real problems — diversity collapse, Sharpe-only myopia, premature convergence — but the cumulative effect on out-of-sample performance was negligible. This article documents what each version attempted, where each failed, and why the next step is not V8 but a fundamentally different approach: automated research that generates hypotheses before evolution begins.

I V5: Diversity Enforcement

V5 addressed the most visible pathology in V1–V4 runs: premature convergence. By generation 10, 90% of the population would share the same leaf signature as the seed champion. Fitness sharing, introduced in V3, was theoretically sound — divide fitness by the square root of niche size — but in practice, the champion's absolute fitness advantage (often 2–3 points) overpowered the sharing penalty.

V5 introduced diversity enforcement via leaf signature tracking, cross-ticker meta-learning, and IV-adjusted delta. While training fitness climbed significantly (AAPL +49%, GOOG +42%), it primarily made evolution better at fitting the training set without improving generalization.

II V6: Multi-Objective Fitness

The central insight motivating V6 was that Sharpe-only fitness rewards consistency over magnitude. The GA was discovering low-risk, low-return trees and calling them optimal. V6 replaced the single-metric fitness with a weighted composite: 60% delta_sharpe, 25% normalized return, and 15% normalized Calmar ratio.

This introduced fitness instability. Champions stored with high fitness under V5 re-evaluated to negative scores under V6. More fundamentally, it replaced one proxy with a weighted average of three proxies, and the forward test confirmed no improved correlation with out-of-sample performance.

III V7: Stagnation Tiers

V7 was the most ambitious revision, attacking premature convergence and isolated evolution through adaptive stagnation tiers and universal champion seeding.

The cross-ticker matrix evaluation showed that "universal" champions (like NVDA and COST) often performed better on other tickers than their native ones, suggesting locally optimal but globally brittle local strategies.

IV The Reality Check

The forward test on 2026 data returned a fitness-PNL correlation of r=0.108. Knowledge of a strategy's training fitness told almost nothing about its forward performance. The system was optimizing a proxy that did not predict the outcome we cared about.

"Training fitness of 3.93 produced a forward correlation of r=0.108. The GA is optimizing a proxy that does not predict the outcome we care about."

Conclusion: Beyond Evolution

The path forward is AutoResearch. Instead of evolving trees and hoping they correspond to valid hypotheses, we must start with hypotheses and generate trees to test them. V8 will integrate forward-validated fitness, hard veto caps, and a persistent research memory to bridge the gap between structure and signal.
